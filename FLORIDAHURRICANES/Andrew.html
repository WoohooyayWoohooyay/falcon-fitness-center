Hurricane Andreww

When it started: August 16th 1992

How it progressed: The hurricane made landfall on Elliott Key, Florida, and then near Homestead on the morning of August 24. The storm continued to move westward into the Gulf of Mexico, where it gradually turned northwestward, bringing Andrew into central Louisiana on August 26 as a Category 3 hurricane.

Date and time of landfall: August 24, 1992

Overall Description: Hurricane Andrew was a Category 5 storm that hit South Florida on August 24th 1992 with wind speeds of 165 miles per hour. The storm caused damage to south Louisiana and the Bahamas, but the brunt of its impact fell on South Florida, where: About 250,000 people were left homeless in Dade County alone.

Anything unique:It's estimated that Andrew spawned more than 28 tornadoes from formation until its dissipation

Cities impacted: Thousands of power outages occurred, especially in East Naples, Everglades City, and Marco Island. Throughout Collier County, Andrew destroyed 80 mobile homes and severely damaged 400 others. Property damage in the county reached about $30 million.
